## Problems data class — Comment Position Summary

**Class composition in v7:**
- **Condition Status** (new in v7) — how a condition presents or manifests in the patient. Examples: active, inactive, recurrence, remission. *(Covered in detail with Procedure Status — recapped here briefly.)*
- **Problems** (existing) — condition, diagnosis, or reason for seeking medical attention. SNOMED CT and ICD-10-CM applicable.
- **Date of Diagnosis** (existing) — date/time when condition was first identified.
- **Date of Resolution** (existing) — date/time when condition was resolved or in remission.
- **SDOH Problems/Health Concerns** (existing) — social determinants of health-related conditions.

| Position | Organizations | Reasons |
|---|---|---|
| **OPPOSE** | *(no outright opposition)* | — |
| **OPPOSE / REDESIGN** | TMA (state physician society) — Condition Status only | Defer Condition Status until vocabulary specified |
| **MIXED / OPPOSE** | Epic (EHR vendor), AMIA (physician informatics professional society) | Epic: Condition Status definition ambiguous. AMIA: Date of Diagnosis/Resolution not useful as currently structured |
| **SUPPORT / with CHANGES** | CSTE (state epidemiologists), Regenstrief Institute (research/informatics), csnewman (individual), AMIA (cited ANI input) | Specify FHIR Condition Clinical Status value set; align Condition Status definition with Procedure/Immunization Status; add "first noted/documented" date concept |
| **SUPPORT** | CMS-CCSQ (federal/payer), Oracle Health (EHR vendor) | Mature; supports longitudinal tracking |

**Comments grouped by position:**

**Strongly supportive**
- **CMS-CCSQ** [federal agency / payer] — Applauds inclusion of Condition Status in USCDI v7. Brief, unqualified support consistent with CMS-CCSQ's pattern across other v7 elements.
- **Oracle Health** [EHR vendor] — Supports inclusion of the proposed Condition Status data element. Brief, unqualified support.

**Supportive with refinements**
- **CSTE (Council of State and Territorial Epidemiologists)** [state public health surveillance] — Supports inclusion of Condition Status. Recommends ONC update the definition to **remove the use of the wording "presents or manifests in the patient."** Frames condition status as critical for case classification and reporting.
- **Regenstrief Institute** [research/informatics institute] — Supports Condition Status. Notes how a condition presents/manifests (active, resolved, recurrence, remission) with no vocabulary specified in draft. Recommends referencing the **FHIR Condition Clinical Status value set** for implementer consistency. Aligned with public health priorities — condition status needed for case classification.
- **csnewman** [unaffiliated individual] — Independent of Epic and CSTE, flags the same wording concern: in the description of Condition Status, the use of "presents or manifests" is odd for a status, evoking more of a phenotypic description (rash, etc.) than a status. Suggests **alignment with the definition of Immunization Status and/or Procedure Status** could be considered.

**Mixed / oppose**
- **Epic** [EHR vendor] — On Condition Status: recommends ONC clarify the definition. The current definition's use of **"presents or manifests"** is ambiguous as a descriptor of status and could be interpreted more broadly than intended. For example, a condition such as hypertension "manifests" through elevated blood pressure readings — entirely distinct from whether the condition is currently active or has been resolved. Additionally, the examples (active, inactive, recurrence) **are not mutually exclusive** — a condition can be an active recurrence, satisfying both categories — and it is unclear whether a condition is expected to carry a single status value or whether multiple statuses can apply simultaneously. Recommends ONC revise the definition and examples to align with existing standards representations of condition status, such as **the clinical status value set defined in the FHIR specification**.
- **AMIA** [physician informatics professional society] — Two distinct critiques on the Problems class as a whole:
  - **On the Problems element itself**: notes that ASTP/ONC's inclusion of SNOMED CT U.S. Edition (March 2022 Release) and ICD-10-CM 2022 as vocabulary standards represents an important step. **However, "in reality, the problem or reason for seeking medical attention is often not documented in the health record."** "Actual Date of Diagnosis" and "Date of Resolution" are typically not known or are captured without adequate context to determine accuracy. **These elements are included but may not yield any accurate information.**
  - **On Date of Diagnosis and Date of Resolution as currently structured**: "Date of Diagnosis and Date of Resolution are not useful as such." Recommends ASTP/ONC consider when (days, weeks, months) first recognized or addressed (Provenance — by whom), and whether resolved or chronic, or the like. Recognize that in the context of an interoperable document or data set, these represent **snapshots in time**. Longitudinal observations and trajectories are not well represented in EHRs.
  - **Sub-recommendation citing ANI input**: under "Problems (Under Problems)," AMIA includes — "Date of Diagnosis and Resolution does not account for the **history of the problem/diagnosis/concern**. Date of **first noted/documented** to show historical care would assist in cleaning up the data for reconciliation process." This proposes a structurally new element distinct from existing Date of Diagnosis (which captures clinical diagnosis) and Date of Resolution (which captures resolution).

**Oppose / redesign**
- **TMA (Texas Medical Association)** [state physician professional society] — Lists Condition Status among 15 elements ONC should not adopt until each has a correlating vocabulary standard. (TMA does not include other Problems class elements in their defer list.)

**Other Problems class commentary:**
- **Specimen Condition vs. Condition and Disposition of Specimens rename**: SHIELD and APHL both file comments on the rename of "Specimen Condition Acceptability" to "Specimen Condition" (covered under the Laboratory class). Note: these comments appear in the Problems class data_name field due to ISP form labeling rather than substantive Problems-class content.

**Summary of Comments:** The Problems data class is dominated by commentary on **Condition Status**, the only v7 addition. The vocabulary and definitional consensus on Condition Status was covered in detail with Procedure Status: three independent voices (Epic, CSTE, csnewman) converge on the same critique that **"presents or manifests"** is the wrong framing for a status field, and Regenstrief points cleanly to the **FHIR Condition Clinical Status value set** as the obvious vocabulary anchor — the same value set used in the FHIR US Core Condition profile. Epic's [EHR vendor] critique is the sharpest: hypertension "manifests" through elevated BP readings, but that's phenotype, not status; and the example values active/inactive/recurrence are not mutually exclusive (an active recurrence satisfies both categories). **The more structurally consequential commentary on the Problems data class as a whole comes from AMIA** [physician informatics professional society], which raises a fundamental data-quality concern that no other v7 commenter raises: **the Problems class elements may be technically correct but not reflective of how diagnosis information is actually documented in EHRs**. AMIA's argument has three layers. First, the problem or reason for seeking medical attention is often not documented in the health record at all — so the Problems element may be empty for many patient encounters. Second, Date of Diagnosis and Date of Resolution are typically not known with adequate context — clinicians document the encounter in which a condition is observed but rarely the actual onset or resolution. Third, longitudinal observations and trajectories are not well represented in EHRs — the Problems class captures snapshots, not the temporal progression that would support meaningful clinical decision-making and care coordination. **AMIA's proposed solution** — citing ANI input — is a new structural element capturing **"date first noted/documented"** to show historical care presence, distinct from a clinical diagnosis date. This would serve data reconciliation use cases (when the same condition appears in records from multiple sources, knowing when each source first noted it helps deduplicate and align longitudinal data) and would acknowledge that EHR documentation timestamps are a different concept from clinical onset dates. **No other commenter engages with this data-quality argument**, but it surfaces a structural critique of USCDI's approach to temporal data that mirrors HL7's Diagnostic Report Date critique (administrative date vs. clinical context date) and Altarum's Performance Time critique. The shared takeaway: **USCDI's date elements across multiple data classes (Problems, Diagnostic Imaging, Healthcare Information Attributes) underspecify the difference between clinical onset/observation timestamps and administrative documentation timestamps**, and AMIA's Problems-class commentary is the most direct articulation of why this matters for data reconciliation and longitudinal care. The absence of broader engagement with the existing Problems class elements — Date of Diagnosis, Date of Resolution, SDOH Problems/Health Concerns — across the rest of the v7 comment record suggests stakeholders are accepting these elements as currently defined, not because they're working well, but because revising them isn't on the v7 table.

**Notably absent:** AHA, FAH, AHIP, AAAAI (notable — allergy/immunology professionals manage chronic conditions extensively), AQIPS, NCQA (notable — HEDIS uses Problems-class data extensively for measure logic but no specific Problems-class comments beyond Condition Status), WEDI, EHR Association (notably did NOT address Condition Status despite addressing Reason Not Performed and Healthcare Information Attributes broadly), MEDITECH, Allina Health, Emory Healthcare, UI Health, Providence Health, FDA, CDC, APHL, CSTE (engaged on Condition Status only), AHIMA (notable — diagnosis coding is core AHIMA professional domain), ACLA, SHIELD, CARIN Alliance, PACIO, **SNOMED International** (notable — they engaged on most other v7 elements with specific code recommendations and Problems is the natural home for SNOMED CT condition concepts), NCPDP, Wolters Kluwer, Vega Health, and Vizient are silent on the Problems class beyond what's been captured. The complete absence of patient advocacy and chronic disease groups (American Diabetes Association, American Heart Association, American Cancer Society) is the most striking gap given that chronic disease management depends entirely on how Problems-class data is captured and exchanged. AHIMA's silence is particularly notable — diagnosis coding accuracy and integrity has been an AHIMA professional priority for decades, and AMIA's data-quality concerns about Date of Diagnosis would naturally invite AHIMA engagement. Pediatric specialty societies are absent despite diagnosis documentation being a major pediatric workflow concern.
