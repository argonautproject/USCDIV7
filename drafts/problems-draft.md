### Condition Status (Problems data class) — Comment Position Summary


**Comments grouped by position:**

**Strongly supportive**
- **CMS-CCSQ** [federal agency / payer] — Applauds inclusion of Condition Status in USCDI v7. Brief, consistent with CMS-CCSQ's pattern across other v7 elements.
- **Oracle Health** [EHR vendor] — Supports inclusion of the proposed Condition Status data element. Brief, unqualified support.

**Supportive with refinements**
- **CSTE (Council of State and Territorial Epidemiologists)** [state public health surveillance] — Supports inclusion. Recommends ONC update the definition to **remove the use of the wording "presents or manifests in the patient."** Frames condition status as critical for case classification and reporting. This is the same definitional concern raised independently by Epic and csnewman.
- **Regenstrief Institute** [research/informatics institute] — Supports inclusion. Notes how a condition presents/manifests (active, resolved, recurrence, remission) with no vocabulary specified in draft. Recommends referencing the **FHIR Condition Clinical Status value set** for implementer consistency. Aligned with public health priorities — condition status needed for case classification.
- **American Medical Association / AMA** [physician professional society] — Combined Procedures and Status Elements section addressing Condition Status alongside Procedure Status and Immunization Status. Supports expansion of status elements that improve interpretability and longitudinal tracking. Recommends ASTP/ONC provide **clear value set guidance and examples to ensure consistent use across systems and settings, especially where status may be inferred from workflow rather than explicitly documented**.
- **csnewman** [unaffiliated individual] — Independent of Epic and CSTE, flags the same wording concern: in the description of Condition Status, the use of "presents or manifests" is odd for a status, evoking more of a phenotypic description (rash, etc.) than a status. Suggests **alignment with the definition of Immunization Status and/or Procedure Status** could be considered.

**Mixed / oppose**
- **Epic** [EHR vendor] — Recommends ONC clarify the definition. The current definition's use of **"presents or manifests"** is ambiguous as a descriptor of status and could be interpreted more broadly than intended. For example, a condition such as hypertension "manifests" through elevated blood pressure readings — entirely distinct from whether the condition is currently active or has been resolved. Additionally, the examples (active, inactive, recurrence) **are not mutually exclusive** — a condition can be an active recurrence, satisfying both categories — and it is unclear whether a condition is expected to carry a single status value or whether multiple statuses can apply simultaneously. Recommends ONC revise the definition and examples to align with existing standards representations of condition status, such as **the clinical status value set defined in the FHIR specification**.

**Oppose / redesign**
- **TMA (Texas Medical Association)** [state physician professional society] — Lists Condition Status among 15 elements ONC should not adopt until each has a correlating vocabulary standard.

**Summary of Comments:** Condition Status is broadly supported as a concept but has a sharper definitional concern than most v7 additions, with three independent voices converging on the same critique. **Epic** [EHR vendor], **CSTE** [state epidemiologists], and **csnewman** [individual] all independently flag that the phrase **"presents or manifests in the patient"** is the wrong framing for a status field. **Epic** frames the critique most precisely: hypertension "manifests" through elevated BP readings, but that's phenotype, not status; and the example values active/inactive/recurrence are not mutually exclusive (an active recurrence satisfies both categories) — leaving implementers uncertain whether a condition carries a single status value or whether multiple statuses can apply simultaneously. **CSTE** independently recommends the same wording be removed, framing condition status as critical for case classification and public-health reporting where ambiguous status values would undermine surveillance accuracy. **csnewman** suggests the cleanest fix: align the Condition Status definition with the Immunization Status and Procedure Status definitions already in v7, since all three are status fields and should follow consistent definitional patterns. The vocabulary recommendation is also clean and unanimous: **Regenstrief Institute** [research/informatics institute] and **Epic** [EHR vendor] both point to the **FHIR Condition Clinical Status value set**, which is the standard answer used in the FHIR US Core Condition profile (active | recurrence | relapse | inactive | remission | resolved). Anchoring Condition Status to this value set would resolve the mutual-exclusivity ambiguity Epic raises — the FHIR clinical status codes are designed to be mutually exclusive at any given point in time. **AMA's** [physician professional society] framing — that ONC should provide value-set guidance "especially where status may be inferred from workflow rather than explicitly documented" — addresses a parallel concern: in clinical practice, condition status often shifts implicitly as encounters progress (a condition documented as active in one note may be implicitly resolved by a later note's silence on it), and without explicit status documentation, downstream consumers can't reliably interpret what's current. **The shared takeaway across all comments** is that ONC's tendency to specify value sets through narrative examples rather than referencing FHIR value sets directly creates the same problem repeatedly — implementers will read the examples as exhaustive or normative even when they're explicitly illustrative, and without anchoring to the FHIR Condition Clinical Status value set, the v7 draft leaves Condition Status semantically underspecified. ONC's adoption path here looks well-defined: revise the definition to remove "presents or manifests" wording, anchor to the FHIR Condition Clinical Status value set, and align the definitional pattern with Immunization Status and Procedure Status. **The opposition is minimal** — TMA's [state physician society] defer-pending-vocabulary position is the only negative voice and is constructive rather than substantive: specifying the FHIR Condition Clinical Status value set would directly address TMA's concern.

**Notably absent:** AHA, FAH (notable — engaged on Procedure Status with a workflow critique but did not address Condition Status, suggesting Condition Status workflow concerns are less acute), AHIP, AMIA (notable — AMIA addressed the broader Problems class with critiques on Date of Diagnosis and Date of Resolution but did not separately engage on Condition Status), ANI, AAAAI (notable — chronic condition status is core to allergy/immunology longitudinal care but no engagement), AQIPS, **NCQA** (the single most striking absence — HEDIS measures extensively use condition status active/resolved/recurrence concepts for measure logic, including diabetes, hypertension, depression, and cancer measures), WEDI, EHR Association (notably did not address Condition Status despite addressing Reason Not Performed and Healthcare Information Attributes broadly), MEDITECH, Allina Health, Emory Healthcare, UI Health, Providence Health, FDA, CDC, APHL, AHIMA (notable — diagnosis coding integrity is core AHIMA professional domain), ACLA, SHIELD, CARIN Alliance, PACIO, **SNOMED International** (notable — they engaged on most other v7 elements with specific SNOMED CT code recommendations and SNOMED CT has rich clinical status concepts that could complement FHIR Condition Clinical Status), NCPDP, Wolters Kluwer, Vega Health, Vizient, and patient advocacy groups are silent on Condition Status despite filing v7 letters. The complete absence of chronic disease patient advocacy organizations (American Diabetes Association, American Heart Association, American Cancer Society) is the most striking gap given that condition status (active, remission, recurrence) is central to chronic disease management and patient-facing health records. NCQA's silence is the single most consequential professional absence — quality measurement is the largest downstream consumer of condition status data, and HEDIS measure construction depends entirely on accurate active/resolved/recurrence distinctions. SNOMED International's absence is also notable given they could have provided specific SNOMED CT clinical finding codes (e.g., 55561003 |Active|, 73425007 |Inactive|, 277022003 |Remission phase|, 263855007 |Recurrent (qualifier value)|) to complement the FHIR Condition Clinical Status value set Regenstrief and Epic recommend.
